Sounak Paul

I am an incoming PhD student in Statistics at the University of Chicago. I did my MSc in Pure Mathematics from University of Alberta, Canada; my Bachelor of Mathematics from Indian Statistical Institute, Bangalore; and my high schooling from Delhi Public School, Ruby Park Kolkata. Please scroll down to learn more about me.

Research Interests

I am currently in the first year of my doctoral program at UChicago. My research interests include Probability Theory, Theoretical Statistics and Functional Analysis, as well as related disciplines like Machine Learning and Pattern Recognition. I have held student research positions in ISI Bangalore and TIFR-CAM. My hobbies include playing the violin, reading superhero stories, and cricket.

Owing to my tender age I ended up being the youngest participant there. I was awe-struck by the plethora of projects, on topics as diverse as robotics and 3d short films. Overall it was a great learning curve and occupies an essential place amidst my childhood memories.

Research Projects

ISI Bangalore KVPY Project

Prof. Yogeshwaran Dhandapani, ISI Bangalore • May 2015 - July 2015

I did a 2 month project under Prof. Yogeshwaran Dhandapani on a non-measure theoretic proof of the Strong Law of Large Numbers and Fekete's Lemma and it's corollaries. I also read the book "Generatingfunctionology" by Herbert Wilf and solved exercises as instructed by my guide.

VSRP at TIFR-CAM

Prof. K Sandeep, TIFR-CAM • May 2016 - July 2016

I worked under Prof. K Sandeep for 2 months. It was primarily an advanced comprehensive reading project on the topics of Differential Geometry, Calculus on Manifolds, Theory of Differential Equations and the beginnings of Functional Analysis. I have not been acquainted with any of these subjects formally before, and hence thoroughly enjoyed this project. I am also solving problems as directed by my guide.

Winter Internship at IISc Bangalore
